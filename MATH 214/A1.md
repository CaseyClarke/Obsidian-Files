# Question 1

(a)
$a_n = 3^{\frac{-2}{\sqrt{n}}}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{-2}{\sqrt{n}} = 0$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}a_n = 3^0 = 1$

(b)
$b_n = \frac{7n^4 +2n + 1}{3 - 5n^2 + 2n^4}$
ignore all but the highest term in the polynomial
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{7n^4}{2n^4} = \frac72$
or more formally 
$b_n = \frac{7 + \frac{2n}{n^4} + \frac{1}{n^4}}{2 + \frac{3}{n^4} \frac{- 5n^2}{n^4}}$
the $n^4$ terms dominate the numerator and vanish
$\lim_{n \to \infty}b_n =\frac72$

(c)
$c_n  = \frac{n^2+3n}{\sqrt{5+n^3}} = \frac{n^2+3n}{\sqrt{n^3(1+ \frac{1}{n^3})}}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}1+\frac{1}{n^3} = 1$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{n(n+3)}{n\sqrt{n}}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{n+3}{\sqrt{n}}$
$n+3 > \sqrt{n} \quad \forall x>0$
DNE ($\infty$)

(d)
$d_n = \sqrt{3n^4 + 5n} - n^2$
$d_n = \sqrt{n^4(3 + \frac{5n}{n^4})} - n^2$
$d_n = n^2\sqrt{(3 + \frac{5}{n^3})} - n^2$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}3 + \frac{5}{n^3} = 3$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}n^2\sqrt3 - n^2$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}d_n = \sqrt3$

(e)
$ln(n)$ converges to $1$ but the $(-1)^n$ term essentially makes it "converge" to oscillating between $-1$ and $1$ forever which is DNE

# Question 3

(a)
$a_n = \frac{2n}{11}\sin(\frac{11}{2n}) = \frac{\sin(\frac{11}{2n})}{\frac{11}{2n}}$
asymptotically the $\frac{11}{2}$ coefficients do nothing
$\lim_{n \to \infty}a_n = \frac{\sin(\frac1n)}{\frac1n}$
Let $x = \frac1n$
as $n \to \infty$ $x \to 0$
so the limit becomes
$\lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\sin(x)}{x}$
this is a famous limit that equals $1$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}a_n = 1$

(b)
$b_n = n^2\tan(\frac{n-1}{5n^3+2n})$
$b_n = n^2(\frac{n-1}{5n^3+2n})\frac{\tan(\frac{n-1}{5n^3+2n})}{\frac{n-1}{5n^3+2n}}$
Let $x = \frac{n-1}{5n^3+2n}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{n-1}{5n^3+2n}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{n(1-\frac{1}{n})}{n(5n^2+2)}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{1-\frac{1}{n}}{5n^2+2}$
the $1$, $-\frac1n$, and $+2$ will all vanish as $n \to \infty$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{1}{5n^2} = 0$
so as $n \to \infty$ $x \to 0$ 
$b_n = n^2x\frac{\tan(x)}{x}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}b_n  = \lim_{x \to 0} n^2x\frac{\tan(x)}{x}$
$\lim_{x \to 0}\frac{\tan(x)}{x}$
the above is a famous limit that goes to $1$ so 
$\lim_{x \to 0} n^2x(1) = \lim_{n \to \infty}n^2(\frac{n-1}{5n^3+2n})$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}(\frac{n^2(n-1)}{n^2(5n+\frac{2n}{n^2})})$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}(\frac{n-1}{5n+\frac{2}{n}})$
the $-1$ and $\frac2n$ terms vanish
$\lim_{n \to \infty}(\frac{n}{5n})$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}(\frac{n}{5n}) = \frac15$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}b_n = \frac15$

(c)
$c_n = (1+\frac{5}{n})^{4n} = ((1+\frac{5}{n})^n)^4$
$(1+\frac{c}{n})^n$ is a famous limit that is defined as 
$\lim_{n \to \infty}(1+\frac{c}{n})^n = e^{c} \quad \forall c \geq 1$
where $e$ is Euler's number
so $c_n$ becomes
$\lim_{n \to \infty}c_n = (e^5)^4$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}c_n = e^{20}$



{{ENDQUESTION}}
