$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{e^{-1}}{\sqrt{n}} = 0$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{e^{1}}{\sqrt{n}} = 0$
by squeeze thm 
$\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{e^{cos(n^2 + 1)}}{\sqrt{n}} = 0$

partial fractions
$\frac{2}{(2n-1)(2n+1)}= \frac{A}{2n-1} + \frac{B}{2n+1}$
$2 = A(2n+1) + B(2n-1)$
$n = 1/2$
$2 = A(2)$
$A = 1$
$n = -1/2$
$2 = B(-2)$
$B = -1$
$\frac{2}{(2n-1)(2n+1)}= \frac{1}{2n-1} - \frac{1}{2n+1}$
$\frac{1}{2n-1} - \frac{1}{2n+1}$ is in the form $g(x) - g(x+1) \quad g(x) = \frac{1}{2x-1}$ making it a telescoping series
writing out the terms we get
$(\frac{1}{1} - \frac{1}{3}) + (\frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{5})  + (\frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{7}) + \dots + (\frac{1}{2n-1} - \frac{1}{2n+1})$
regrouping we get
$1 +  (- \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3}) + (-\frac{1}{5}  + \frac{1}{5}) + (- \frac{1}{7} + \frac{1}{7})+ \dots  - \frac{1}{2n+1}$
all of the terms in the middle cancel out to 0 leaving the whole sequence as 
$1 - \frac{1}{2n+1}$
$\lim_{n \to \infty}1 - \frac{1}{2n+1} = 1$
so the series converges to 1
